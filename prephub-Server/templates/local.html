One primary purpose of the PREPhub is to communicate key messages related
to both disaster preparation as well as to support systems available soon
after a disaster takes place.

Key messages to be shared with PREPhub “interactors” may include:

PREPhub installation is an important element of the city’s BEECN system to support
residents post-disaster.

Come to the PREPhub site for support and information following disaster incidents.

Learn how to get involved through voluntary action. Provide information to visitors
about how they can become volunteers in disaster preparation and response efforts in
their community (e.g. NET, BEECN, CERT).

Click here to learn about becoming a City of Portland NET (Neighborhood Emergency Team)
Volunteer.

Click here to learn about becoming a BEECN (Basic Earthquake Emergency Communication Node)
volunteer.

and first aid supplies for a minimum of two weeks. (Could include referral to sources where
those with low incomes can receive support in putting together their kit.

What does it mean for businesses and agencies?
Businesses and organizations can create resilience plans which include strategies
to be prepared to re-open following a disaster incident. These are often called
“continuity of operations plans.”
